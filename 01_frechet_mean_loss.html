01 — Fréchet-Mean Anchor Loss

For each concept triple (s, v, t) on S^{d−1}, compute the Fréchet/Karcher mean m and pull all three modalities toward it: the anchor is the group’s own consensus, so no modality is privileged (paper.md §2.1). Used as a regularizer on top of the same pairwise-InfoNCE base as every other notebook, so the comparison isolates the geometry.

Design choices (flagged): the anchor is detached (pull-toward-current-consensus); karcher_exact=False switches to the normalized-arithmetic-mean approximation, which the sanity cell below shows is near-exact for tight clusters.

CLI: python run.py 01 → results in runs/01_frechet_mean_loss/<timestamp>/.

Sanity: normalized mean vs. iterative Karcher mean

# For tight clusters the normalized arithmetic mean approximates the Karcher mean
# to 2nd order; verify the iterative version agrees (paper.md §2.1)
import torch
from lib import sphere

base = torch.randn(256, 1, 512)
pts = sphere.normalize(base + 0.15 * torch.randn(256, 3, 512))   # 256 tight triples
approx = sphere.karcher_mean(pts, exact=False)
exact = sphere.karcher_mean(pts, exact=True)
gap = torch.rad2deg(sphere.geodesic_distance(approx, exact))
print(f"angle(approx, exact) over 256 tight triples:  max {gap.max():.5f}°,  mean {gap.mean():.5f}°")
