02 — Spherical-Triangle Area Regularizer

s, v, t are vertices of a geodesic triangle on S^{d−1}; its area = spherical excess E = A + B + C − π (Girard), computed stably from side lengths via L’Huilier’s theorem (paper.md §2.2). E → 0 as the triple approaches a common great circle, so minimizing E couples the three angles that a pairwise-InfoNCE sum optimizes independently.

Key caveat: E = 0 means coplanar-with-origin, not close — this is strictly a shape regularizer and rides on the same pairwise-InfoNCE base as the other notebooks.

CLI: python run.py 02 → results in runs/02_spherical_triangle_loss/<timestamp>/.

Sanity: known areas

import math
import torch
from lib import sphere

# Octant triangle (three orthogonal unit vectors) has excess exactly pi/2
e = torch.eye(3)
E_oct = sphere.spherical_triangle_excess(e[0:1], e[1:2], e[2:3])
print(f"octant triangle:        E = {E_oct.item():.6f}   (exact pi/2 = {torch.pi / 2:.6f})")

# Near-degenerate (three nearly coincident points) -> E ~ 0.
# NB: in 512-dim, eps*randn(512) has norm eps*sqrt(512) — keep eps small.
a = sphere.normalize(torch.randn(1, 512))
b = sphere.normalize(a + 0.004 * torch.randn(1, 512))
c = sphere.normalize(a - 0.006 * torch.randn(1, 512))
print(f"near-coincident triple: E = {sphere.spherical_triangle_excess(a, b, c).item():.3e}   (-> 0)")

# Points on one great circle (coplanar with origin) -> E ~ 0 even though far apart
u, w = torch.linalg.qr(torch.randn(512, 2)).Q.T
gc = lambda t: (math.cos(t) * u + math.sin(t) * w).unsqueeze(0)
print(f"great-circle triple:    E = {sphere.spherical_triangle_excess(gc(0.1), gc(0.9), gc(2.0)).item():.3e}   (-> 0, though points are far apart!)")

near-coincident triple: E = 6.154e-03   (-> 0)
great-circle triple:    E = 5.876e-04   (-> 0, though points are far apart!)
